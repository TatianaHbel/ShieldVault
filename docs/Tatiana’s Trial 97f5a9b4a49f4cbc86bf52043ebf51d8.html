Brief

ShielPay has pivoted into something more retail, and gets very inspired by Revolut and n26, it’s now a blockchain-backed payment app

  • what changed:
    • ShieldPay should firstly focus on mobile
    • It’s not based on EOA anymore: It’s still doing some wallet transactions, shielding, etc under the hood but does not require wallet validations from the user.
    • thus: users now connect by email/google account
    • It’s connected to VISA and Apple Pay, so you can pay in USD and EURO from your USDC account (the mechanism is hidden)
    • It only supports USDC
    • Liquidity is automatically yielding earnings (like in n26)
  • what didn’t change
    • It’s based on blockchain and ensures amount confidentiality
    • the name, it’s still ShieldPay

Design the new onboarding

Users onboarding should fill up the registration form and transfer a minimum of USD50 to their new bank account

  1. The registration form asks for the classical stuff: identity, phone number, postal address, verified email address, valid passport picture, agreeing on ToS
  1. For the entry amount: there are 2 main streams
  • we can onboard users owning USDC or other crypto
  • and users onboarding with fiat (we can support bank transfer or onramp by credit card)

These $50 are then available in their account

They can chose between VISA PREMIER credit card (free) and a VISA PREMIUM ($2/mo) (includes travel insurance, higher spending limits…)

Design the new auto-yield system (bonus)

Every month, users receive a small yield based on how much money was on their account. There is no saving account, the user’s payment account automatically generates a small yield.

  • how is this yield system presented and called
  • users should be able to choose their yield platform (default is AAVE USD Coin, but there is a few (5) alternatives vaults from Morpho or AAVE with different APY
